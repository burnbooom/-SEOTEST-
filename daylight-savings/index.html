What is Daylight Savings Time?

Daylight Saving Time (DST) is the practice of moving clocks forward one hour during warmer months so that evening daylight lasts longer. In the U.S., clocks spring forward in March and fall back in November.

DST by Region

Not all countries observe DST. The European Union follows a different schedule, typically beginning DST on the last Sunday in March and ending on the last Sunday in October.
