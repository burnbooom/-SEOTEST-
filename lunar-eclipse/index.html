What is a Lunar Eclipse?

A lunar eclipse occurs when Earth passes between the Sun and the Moon, casting a shadow on the Moon's surface. During a total lunar eclipse, the Moon often appears red — known as a "blood moon."

Blood Moon

A "blood moon" refers to the reddish appearance of the Moon during a total lunar eclipse. This red color is caused by Rayleigh scattering — the same phenomenon that creates red sunrises and sunsets.
