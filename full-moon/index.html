What is a Full Moon?

A full moon occurs when the Moon is on the opposite side of Earth from the Sun, with its entire face illuminated. It happens approximately once per lunar month (about 29.5 days).

Full Moon Names

Traditional full moon names come from Native American tribes, Colonial American settlers, and other cultures. Each name reflects the seasons and natural events typical during that time of year.
