Why Do We Have Leap Years?

A leap year adds an extra day to the calendar to keep it synchronized with Earth's orbit around the Sun. Without it, the calendar would drift about 1 day every 4 years, eventually shifting the seasons.

Leap Year Rules

A year is a leap year if:

  • Divisible by 4, AND
  • NOT divisible by 100, UNLESS
  • Also divisible by 400

For example, 1900 was not a leap year, but 2000 was.
